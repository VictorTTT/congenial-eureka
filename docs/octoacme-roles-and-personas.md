# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Facilitates agile ceremonies, removes impediments, and coaches the team on agile practices. Acts as a servant-leader to ensure the team can work effectively.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, retrospectives, and sprint reviews
- Identify and remove process impediments that slow team delivery
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Shield the team from external interruptions during sprints
- Track sprint health and flag scope creep or velocity issues

### Goals
- Maintain team flow and sustainable pace
- Continuously improve team agile maturity
- Keep ceremonies focused and time-boxed

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Impediment logs and escalation to PM
- Retrospective facilitation and action item tracking

### Interacts with
Project Manager, Developers, QA, Product Manager

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
Shapes the user experience and interface design of the product. Ensures solutions are usable, accessible, and aligned with customer needs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Contribute to design reviews and product discovery sessions
- Collaborate with developers on feasibility and implementation of designs
- Maintain and evolve the design system or style guide

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce rework caused by late-stage UX changes
- Align visual design with product goals and brand standards

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with Product Manager and Developers
- Annotated wireframes and prototypes shared via design tools
- User research summaries and usability test reports

### Interacts with
Product Manager, Developers, QA

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Provides data-driven insights to inform product decisions and measure project outcomes. Partners with product and engineering to define and track key metrics.

### Responsibilities
- Analyze product usage data and surface actionable insights
- Help define success metrics and KPIs for new features
- Build and maintain dashboards for project and product health
- Support A/B testing design and result interpretation
- Identify anomalies or trends that require attention

### Goals
- Enable data-informed prioritization and decision-making
- Ensure metrics are correctly instrumented and trustworthy
- Reduce reliance on anecdotal evidence in planning

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metric reviews with Product Manager and PM
- Dashboard links and data summaries in status updates
- Ad-hoc analysis reports for major decisions

### Interacts with
Product Manager, Project Manager, Developers

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Drafts, maintains, and improves technical and process documentation. Ensures that knowledge is accessible, clear, and up to date for all team members and stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain user-facing and internal documentation
- Translate complex technical concepts into clear, concise language
- Collaborate with developers and PMs to capture processes and decisions
- Review documentation as part of the Definition of Done
- Support onboarding by maintaining a knowledge base

### Goals
- Reduce knowledge silos and single-person dependencies
- Accelerate onboarding for new team members
- Ensure documentation is versioned, searchable, and accurate

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with all teams
- Pull requests and comments in the docs repository
- Onboarding guides and process walkthroughs

### Interacts with
All roles — central to onboarding and knowledge sharing

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Orchestrates the release process from planning to post-deploy verification. Ensures releases are coordinated, communicated, and executed safely.

### Responsibilities
- Own and maintain the release calendar and deployment schedule
- Coordinate release readiness across development, QA, and operations
- Ensure release checklists and rollback plans are complete before deployment
- Communicate release status and notes to stakeholders and support teams
- Lead post-release verification and incident response coordination

### Goals
- Reduce release risk through standardized processes
- Ensure all stakeholders are informed before and after deployments
- Minimize rollback events through thorough pre-release validation

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews with PM, Developers, and QA
- Deployment notifications and release notes to stakeholders
- Post-deploy status updates and incident coordination

### Interacts with
Project Manager, Developers, QA, Stakeholders

---

## Compliance Lead

### Role Summary
Ensures that projects meet regulatory, security, and legal requirements. Acts as a guardrail to protect the organization and customers.

### Responsibilities
- Identify applicable regulatory, security, and legal requirements for each project
- Review designs, features, and processes for compliance gaps
- Define compliance acceptance criteria and validation steps
- Coordinate audits, certifications, or legal reviews as needed
- Maintain compliance documentation and evidence artifacts

### Goals
- Prevent compliance issues from reaching production
- Reduce legal and regulatory risk for the organization
- Ensure the team understands and applies compliance requirements early

### Typical Communication
- Compliance briefings at project initiation and key milestones
- Review comments on PRs, designs, and release plans
- Audit reports and compliance checklists

### Interacts with
Project Manager, Developers, QA, Legal/Security teams

---

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- The full set of nine personas — Developers, Product Managers, Project Managers, Scrum Master, UX/UI Designer, Data Analyst, Technical Writer, Release Manager, and Compliance Lead — covers the breadth of roles found in cross-functional delivery teams.
